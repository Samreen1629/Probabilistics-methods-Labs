# Task 5 — Buffon's Needle Experiment

## Useful Definitions

**Continuous Sample Space**

A sample space containing infinitely many possible outcomes.

**Elementary Outcome**

A single possible result of an experiment.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

## 1. Sample Space

The outcome of a needle throw depends on

- its **distance from the nearest line**
- its **orientation angle**

---

## 2. Parameters Determining the Outcome

Two parameters describe the needle:

- \(x\) — distance from the center of the needle to the nearest line
- \(\theta\) — angle between the needle and the lines

---

## 3. Representation of an Elementary Outcome

An elementary outcome can be written as

$$
(x,\theta)
$$

where

- \(x\) represents the distance
- \(\theta\) represents the angle

---

## 4. Sample Space

Using symmetry we restrict to

$$
0 \le x \le \frac{d}{2}
$$

and

$$
0 \le \theta \le \frac{\pi}{2}
$$

Thus

$$
\Omega = \{(x,\theta) \mid x \in [0,\tfrac{d}{2}],\ \theta \in [0,\tfrac{\pi}{2}]\}
$$

---

## 5. Why the Sample Space is Continuous

The variables \(x\) and \(\theta\) can take **any real value in their intervals**.

Therefore the sample space contains **infinitely many outcomes**, unlike the previous experiments where the outcomes were **finite and discrete**.
